## Delta Star, Inc. (Booth 300)

**Website:** https://www.deltastar.com

### Company Overview
Delta Star is one of the largest manufacturers of medium-power and large distribution transformers in North America. Founded in 1908, the company has over a century of experience in the transformer industry. They specialize in custom-engineered power transformers for electric utilities, industrial facilities, and renewable energy applications.

**Headquarters:** Lynchburg, Virginia
**Manufacturing Facilities:** Lynchburg, VA and San Carlos, CA

### Products & Services
- **Medium Power Transformers:** 10 MVA to 250 MVA, up to 230 kV class
- **Large Distribution Transformers:** Substation-class transformers
- **Mobile Transformers/Substations:** Trailer-mounted units for emergency deployment
- **Renewable Energy Transformers:** Specifically designed for solar and wind farm collector substations
- **Phase-Shifting Transformers**
- **Auto-Transformers**
- **Reactor products**
- **Transformer repair and refurbishment services**
- **After-market parts and field services**

### Team/Leadership
- Historically a privately-held company
- Part of the broader transformer manufacturing sector leadership
- Known for employing highly specialized transformer design engineers

### Technology Stack
- Custom transformer design engineering
- Advanced insulation systems
- Oil-filled and dry-type transformer technologies
- Computer-aided design and testing facilities
- Factory acceptance testing (FAT) capabilities

### Market Positioning
- **Competitors:** ABB (Hitachi Energy), Siemens Energy, WEG, Howard Industries, Prolec-GE, SPX Transformer Solutions, ERMCO (also at this conference)
- **Target Audience:** Electric utilities (IOUs, co-ops, munis), renewable energy developers, industrial customers
- **Pricing Model:** Custom-engineered, project-based pricing; lead times have been historically long (12-24+ months)
- **Positioning:** Premium domestic manufacturer with deep engineering expertise; known for quality and custom solutions

### Recent News (2024-2025)
- The U.S. transformer market has been under extreme supply pressure due to grid modernization, renewable energy growth, and aging infrastructure replacement
- Industry-wide transformer lead times reached 2-3+ years
- The Biden administration's infrastructure investments (IIJA) and Inflation Reduction Act (IRA) have driven significant demand
- Domestic transformer manufacturers including Delta Star have been expanding capacity
- DOE efficiency standards updates have affected product design requirements

### Relation to Power Utilities & Low Voltage Regulation
**HIGHLY RELEVANT.** Delta Star is a core transformer supplier to electric cooperatives and other utilities. Their products are directly involved in voltage transformation and power distribution. Medium power transformers are used in substations that step voltage down for distribution.

### Renewable Energy Connections
**HIGHLY RELEVANT.** Delta Star actively manufactures transformers specifically designed for solar and wind farm applications, including collector substation transformers and step-up transformers for renewable generation facilities.

## ERMCO (Booth 1017)

**Website:** https://www.ermco.com

### Company Overview
ERMCO (Electric Research & Manufacturing Cooperative, Inc.) is a unique entity in the electric utility industry -- it is a cooperative-owned transformer manufacturer. ERMCO is owned by electric distribution cooperatives and manufactures distribution transformers specifically for the cooperative market. This gives ERMCO a distinctive market position as a cooperative serving cooperatives.

**Headquarters:** Dyersburg, Tennessee
**Founded:** 1968
**Ownership:** Owned by approximately 700+ member electric cooperatives
**Manufacturing:** Multiple manufacturing facilities in Tennessee

### Products & Services
- **Pole-Mounted Distribution Transformers:** Single-phase and three-phase
- **Pad-Mounted Distribution Transformers:** Loop-feed and radial-feed configurations
- **Underground Distribution Transformers:** Submersible and vault-type
- **Three-Phase Transformers:** For commercial and industrial applications
- **Transformer Accessories:** Bushings, fuses, connectors
- **Mini Pad-Mounted Transformers**
- **CSP (Completely Self-Protected) Transformers**

### Team/Leadership
- Governed by a board of directors elected from member cooperatives
- CEO/GM manages day-to-day operations
- Strong alignment with NRECA and the cooperative utility model

### Technology Stack
- Modern transformer manufacturing with automated winding equipment
- Advanced core materials (amorphous and grain-oriented silicon steel)
- DOE 2016 and upcoming DOE efficiency standard compliant designs
- Quality testing per IEEE/ANSI standards

### Market Positioning
- **Competitors:** ABB (Hitachi Energy), Eaton (Cooper Power), Howard Industries, Prolec-GE, WEG, Elsewedy, Delta Star (also at this conference)
- **Target Audience:** EXCLUSIVELY electric cooperatives (member-owners)
- **Pricing Model:** Member pricing; cooperative ownership structure provides cost advantages
- **Positioning:** The only cooperative-owned transformer manufacturer in the U.S. This is a major differentiator. Cooperatives that are member-owners get preferential pricing and allocation -- critical during the ongoing transformer shortage.

### Recent News (2024-2025)
- ERMCO has been at the center of the transformer shortage crisis, as cooperative demand for distribution transformers has far outstripped supply
- Significant investments in manufacturing capacity expansion
- Lead times for distribution transformers industry-wide have been 1-3+ years
- ERMCO's cooperative ownership model gives member co-ops priority access to supply
- DOE efficiency standards debate has affected transformer design and material costs
- Amorphous metal core transformers gaining traction for higher efficiency

### Online Sentiment
- Highly valued by the cooperative community as "their own" manufacturer
- Some frustration industry-wide about transformer lead times, but ERMCO members get priority
- NRECA has advocated on behalf of cooperatives regarding DOE transformer efficiency standards

### Relation to Power Utilities & Low Voltage Regulation
**CRITICAL RELEVANCE.** ERMCO's distribution transformers are the fundamental equipment for voltage transformation at the service level. Proper transformer sizing and tap settings are essential for maintaining voltage within ANSI standards at customer premises. Every cooperative distribution system relies on transformers like those ERMCO manufactures.

### Renewable Energy Connections
**RELEVANT.** Distribution transformers are needed for distributed solar interconnection (step-up from inverter voltage to distribution voltage) and for general system capacity as renewable energy drives load growth and bidirectional power flows.
