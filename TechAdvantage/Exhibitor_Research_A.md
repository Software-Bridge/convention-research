## 1. ABB Electrification (Booth 1734)

**Website**: https://electrification.us.abb.com/

### Company Overview
ABB Electrification is the electrification business area of ABB Ltd, a Swiss-Swedish multinational corporation. ABB Electrification is one of the world's largest suppliers of electrical distribution and power management products, solutions, and services. The division was formally structured after ABB's strategic reorganization (completed ~2020) into four business areas: Electrification, Motion, Process Automation, and Robotics & Discrete Automation.

ABB Electrification focuses on the full electrical value chain from substation to socket. Their product portfolio includes:
- **Medium Voltage (MV) switchgear and equipment**: Ring main units, reclosers, sectionalizers, circuit breakers
- **Low Voltage (LV) products**: Circuit breakers, switches, contactors, enclosures
- **Power distribution transformers**: Including pad-mounted, pole-mounted, and substation transformers
- **Voltage regulators**: Step voltage regulators for distribution networks
- **Power quality solutions**: Capacitor banks, power factor correction, harmonic filters
- **Smart grid solutions**: Distribution automation, SCADA integration, ADMS (Advanced Distribution Management Systems)
- **EV charging infrastructure**: Through their ABB E-mobility division
- **Solar inverters**: Previously through Power-One/ABB, now through FIMER (divested)
- **Electrical installation products**: Under brands like Thomas & Betts

### **CRITICAL FLAG -- DIRECT COMPETITOR/PARTNER TO PACVOLT**
ABB manufactures step voltage regulators and distribution transformers that are directly relevant to PacVolt's LVR product line. ABB's voltage regulators are widely deployed across electric cooperatives and municipal utilities. They are likely one of the largest competitors in the voltage regulation space, though their focus tends to be on larger (higher kVA) regulators rather than the low-voltage niche PacVolt may occupy.

### Team/Leadership
- **ABB Group CEO**: Bjorn Rosengren (stepped down 2024); succeeded by **Morten Wierod** (effective July 2024)
- **ABB Electrification President**: **Morten Wierod** (prior to becoming Group CEO); the Electrification division leadership has been restructured since
- **ABB Electrification North America**: Operations headquartered in multiple US locations including Cary, NC and various manufacturing sites

### Technology Stack
- Proprietary SCADA and DMS platforms
- ABB Ability (IoT/cloud platform for industrial applications)
- IEC 61850 communications protocols
- DNP3.0 protocol support for utility communications
- Cloud-based analytics and asset management tools

### Market Positioning
- **Competitors**: Siemens Energy, Schneider Electric, Eaton, GE Vernova (formerly GE Grid Solutions), Hitachi Energy, S&C Electric
- **Target Audience**: Electric utilities (IOUs, cooperatives, municipals), industrial facilities, commercial buildings, data centers
- **Pricing Model**: Enterprise/project-based pricing; products range from hundreds to millions of dollars depending on scale
- ABB is positioned as a premium, full-spectrum provider -- they can supply everything from a single circuit breaker to a complete substation

### Recent News (2024-2025)
- ABB completed its spin-off of the turbocharging division (Accelleron) in 2022
- Morten Wierod became CEO of ABB Group in July 2024
- ABB has been investing heavily in EV charging infrastructure globally
- Continued expansion of smart grid and grid-edge solutions
- ABB Electrification reported strong revenue growth in 2023-2024, driven by electrification trends and data center demand

### Online Sentiment
Generally very positive in the utility industry. ABB is seen as a reliable, technology-forward supplier. Some criticism around pricing (premium) and complexity of their product lines. Strong reputation for engineering quality.
