## Eaton (Cooper Power Systems) — Booth 1825
**Website**: https://www.eaton.com/cooperpower

### Company Overview
Eaton's Cooper Power Systems division is one of the primary suppliers of voltage regulators, reclosers, capacitor controllers, and distribution automation equipment to electric cooperatives. Through the Cooper Power heritage (Eaton acquired Cooper Industries in 2012 for ~$13B), they have decades of deep relationships with rural electric cooperatives.

### Products (Voltage Regulation)
- **VR-32 Step Voltage Regulator**: The most widely deployed single-phase step voltage regulator in North America
- **EVER-Tap**: Solid-state tap changer technology (next-gen replacement for mechanical regulators)
- **CL-7 Electronic Recloser Controller**: Includes voltage regulation control capability
- **Yukon SCADA Platform**: Integrated with their voltage regulation equipment
- **Distribution Automation**: S&C IntelliRupter PulseCloser, overhead/underground switches

### Leadership
- **CEO (Eaton)**: Craig Arnold
- **Electrical Sector President**: Revathi Advaithi (until 2024); leadership has rotated
- Eaton Power Distribution Solutions often represented at cooperative conferences by dedicated co-op sales team

### Technology Stack
- DNP3.0 and IEC 61850 protocols
- Yukon SCADA/DMS platform
- Capacitor control automation
- Power Quality monitoring

### Market Positioning
- **Market share**: Estimated 40-50% of cooperative voltage regulator market (VR-32)
- **Revenue (Eaton overall)**: ~$23B (2024)
- **Key Strength**: Installed base — thousands of VR-32s already in cooperative systems
- **Pricing**: Premium; VR-32 regulators typically $15,000-$30,000+ each
- **Weakness**: Mechanical tap changers have maintenance requirements; the EVER-Tap solid-state is newer and more expensive

### Recent News (2024-2025)
- Eaton reported strong 2024 earnings driven by electrification and data center demand
- Continued investment in grid modernization and EV charging infrastructure
- Expanded manufacturing capacity for grid equipment due to Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act (IIJA) demand
- Cooper Power Systems product line continues to be core to cooperative market

### Online Sentiment
Very strong reputation for reliability. Some cooperative engineers note that VR-32 pricing has increased significantly. EVER-Tap technology is viewed with interest but adoption is still early.

### Relevance to PacVolt
**CRITICAL COMPETITOR**: Eaton's VR-32 is the benchmark product PacVolt competes against. Understanding their pricing, lead times, EVER-Tap roadmap, and cooperative sales strategy is essential. Key intelligence targets: EVER-Tap pricing vs. LVR, current VR-32 lead times, new product announcements.

## Reinhausen Manufacturing — Booth 209
**Website**: https://www.reinhausen.com

### Company Overview
Maschinenfabrik Reinhausen (MR) is the global leader in on-load tap-changers (OLTCs) and voltage regulation technology. The Regensburg, Germany-based company holds approximately 50% global market share in tap-changers and is the single most important company in the voltage regulation supply chain worldwide.

### Products (Voltage Regulation)
- **VACUTAP**: Vacuum-type on-load tap-changer (flagship product line)
- **TAPCON**: Tap-changer control and monitoring systems
- **ETOS**: Energy Transformer Operating System — digital platform for transformer monitoring and voltage regulation optimization
- **GRIDCON**: Power quality and reactive power compensation systems
- **MSENSE**: Dissolved gas analysis and transformer monitoring

### Leadership
- **Family-owned** (Scheubeck family, 5th generation)
- **CEO**: Nicolas Maier-Scheubeck
- US subsidiary: Reinhausen Manufacturing Inc., headquartered in the United States
- Strong R&D focus — one of the few companies with dedicated voltage regulation research

### Technology Stack
- Proprietary VACUTAP vacuum switching technology
- IEC 61850 integration
- ETOS digital platform for IoT-connected transformers
- Advanced dissolved gas analysis (DGA) monitoring
- Digital twin technology for transformer fleets

### Market Positioning
- **Global market leader** in on-load tap-changers (~50% market share)
- **Revenue**: ~€1B+ (estimated, private company)
- **Key Strength**: Technology leadership — their tap-changers are inside transformers from ABB, Siemens, Hitachi, etc.
- **Pricing**: Premium technology pricing
- **Cooperative Market**: Reinhausen's products are typically inside larger substation transformers; their presence at TechAdvantage signals interest in the cooperative distribution market

### Recent News (2024-2025)
- Expanded ETOS digital platform capabilities
- Growing focus on distribution-level voltage regulation (not just transmission)
- Investments in US manufacturing and service capacity
- Increased interest in voltage regulation for renewable energy integration

### Online Sentiment
Extremely high reputation for quality and innovation. Considered the gold standard in tap-changer technology. Some concern about pricing relative to alternatives.

### Relevance to PacVolt
**CRITICAL — POTENTIAL COMPETITOR AND POTENTIAL PARTNER**: Reinhausen's ETOS platform and increasing focus on distribution-level regulation could make them a competitor. However, they could also be a technology partner — their tap-changer expertise could complement PacVolt's LVR products. Key discussion: Are they moving into the low-voltage regulation space directly?
