**DIRECTLY RELEVANT - POTENTIAL COMPETITOR AND/OR PARTNER.** T&R Electric manufactures and remanufactures step-type voltage regulators, which are the traditional solution for voltage regulation on distribution feeders. ## Must-Visit Booths (in priority order):

### 1. Virtual Peaker (Booth 400) — BOOTH NEIGHBOR!
**Why:** They are literally next to PacVolt (Booth 404). Virtual Peaker manages DER (solar, storage, EVs) that cause the voltage issues PacVolt's LVR solves. Immediate partnership opportunity for joint cooperative solutions.

### 2. T&R Electric Supply (Booth 1749) — VOLTAGE REGULATOR MANUFACTURER
**Why:** T&R Electric is the closest competitor/complementary player at the show. They make primary-side step-type voltage regulators; PacVolt makes secondary-side LVR. Understanding their product positioning, customer base, and messaging is critical. Explore whether a complementary partnership (primary + secondary regulation) makes sense.

### 3. Tantalus Systems (Booth 735) — VOLTAGE MONITORING PARTNER
**Why:** Tantalus's TRUVolt product specifically monitors voltage. Their data can identify where LVR products are needed. A "monitor + fix" partnership is compelling for cooperatives.

### 4. Ubicquia (Booth 2245) — VOLTAGE MONITORING PARTNER
**Why:** Ubicquia's UbiVolt/UbiGrid provides voltage monitoring through streetlight infrastructure. Same "monitor + fix" partnership opportunity as Tantalus but through a different monitoring approach.

### 5. Uticom Systems (Booth 1307) — VVO/SCADA PARTNER
**Why:** Uticom's Volt/VAR optimization and SCADA systems coordinate voltage regulation on the distribution system. Integrating PacVolt's LVR into their VVO schemes extends optimization to the secondary level.

### 6. Xylem/Sensus (Booth 1735) — AMI DATA PARTNER
**Why:** Sensus smart meters report voltage data at every customer point. This data identifies locations needing LVR deployment. Massive installed base in the cooperative market.

### 7. Touchstone Energy (Booth 1145) — COOPERATIVE CHANNEL
**Why:** The cooperative industry's national brand. Understanding their programs and getting visibility within the Touchstone Energy ecosystem reaches 700+ cooperatives.

### 8. Texas Electric Cooperatives (Booth 225) — MARKET CHANNEL
**Why:** Texas cooperatives serve vast geographic areas with long rural feeders — ideal candidates for LVR products. The Texas association is a key channel to these cooperatives.
