## 1. Base Power (Booth 2452)

**Website**: https://www.basepower.com/

### Company Overview
Base Power is an energy technology startup focused on distributed energy resources and residential battery storage for grid support. Founded in Texas, Base Power deploys home battery systems that aggregate into virtual power plants (VPPs), providing grid reliability services to utilities. Their model involves installing batteries in homes at no upfront cost to the homeowner, then monetizing the batteries by providing grid services (demand response, peak shaving, frequency regulation) to utilities and grid operators.

Base Power emerged in the Texas market following the devastating Winter Storm Uri (February 2021) that highlighted critical grid reliability failures. The company's approach directly addresses grid resilience by placing distributed energy storage at the edge of the distribution network.

Key products/services include:
- **Residential battery storage systems**: Installed in homes as distributed grid assets
- **Virtual Power Plant (VPP) platform**: Aggregates distributed batteries for coordinated grid services
- **Demand response programs**: Utility-facing programs for peak demand management
- **Grid resilience services**: Emergency backup and grid stabilization capabilities
- **Utility partnership programs**: Custom programs for electric cooperatives and utilities

### **FLAG -- HIGHLY RELEVANT TO PACVOLT**
Base Power's distributed battery storage technology directly relates to voltage regulation challenges on distribution grids. Battery systems injecting/absorbing power at the grid edge create voltage fluctuation issues that PacVolt's LVR products are designed to address. Additionally, as cooperatives adopt DER and battery programs, they will need enhanced voltage regulation equipment. Base Power could be both a driver of demand for PacVolt products and a potential technology integration partner.

### Team/Leadership
- Base Power was founded by energy industry entrepreneurs with backgrounds in power systems and technology
- The company has attracted venture capital funding and energy industry investment
- Specific leadership names should be verified on their website

### Technology Stack
- Cloud-based VPP management platform
- IoT connectivity for battery monitoring and dispatch
- Grid integration protocols (IEEE 2030, OpenADR)
- Machine learning for predictive dispatch optimization
- Real-time telemetry and SCADA integration capabilities

### Market Positioning
- **Competitors**: Tesla (Powerwall/Virtual Power Plant), Sunrun (grid services), Swell Energy, Generac (PWRcell), Enphase (IQ Batteries)
- **Target Audience**: Electric utilities, cooperatives, homeowners in deregulated/stressed grid markets
- **Pricing Model**: No-cost-to-homeowner model; revenue from utility grid service contracts
- Positioned as a grid resilience solution rather than a consumer product

### Recent News (2024-2025)
- Base Power has been expanding operations in the Texas ERCOT market
- Growing interest from electric cooperatives looking for grid resilience solutions
- Venture capital funding rounds to support expansion
- Participation in NRECA events suggests push into the cooperative market

### Online Sentiment
Positive reception in the energy technology and cleantech communities. The no-cost homeowner model is attractive. Some questions about long-term business model sustainability and scalability. The grid resilience angle resonates strongly post-Winter Storm Uri.

### Relation to Power Utilities & Low Voltage Regulation
**HIGHLY RELEVANT**: Distributed battery storage creates voltage regulation challenges on distribution feeders. When batteries charge (absorb power) or discharge (inject power), they cause voltage swings that must be managed. PacVolt's LVR products could be essential companion technology for cooperatives deploying Base Power's battery systems.
