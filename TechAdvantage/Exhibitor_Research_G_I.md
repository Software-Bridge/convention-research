## Gridco Systems (Booth 504)

**RELEVANCE TO PACVOLT**: **EXTREMELY HIGH** -- Direct competitor in voltage regulation for power distribution.

### Company Overview
- **Founded**: 2008, Woburn, Massachusetts [VERIFY]
- **Website**: https://www.gridcosystems.com
- **What they do**: Gridco Systems (or Gridco) develops power electronics-based solutions for electric distribution grids. They are known for their Empower platform, a next-generation in-line power regulator (ILPR) that provides real-time voltage regulation using power electronics rather than traditional tap-changing transformers.

### Products & Services
- **InlineRegulator (ILPR)**: Power electronics-based voltage regulator that provides continuous, step-less voltage regulation (unlike traditional step-type regulators that adjust in discrete tap steps)
- **Empower Platform**: Grid-edge voltage regulation and power flow control
- Key differentiator: Their regulators use power electronics to provide smooth, continuous voltage adjustment rather than mechanical tap changers

### Leadership [VERIFY]
- **CEO**: Information needs verification for current leadership
- Founded by engineers from the power electronics space

### Technology Stack
- Power electronics (IGBT/SiC-based inverter technology)
- Real-time embedded control systems
- Grid-edge computing and analytics
- SCADA/DNP3 integration

### Market Positioning
- **DIRECT COMPETITOR TO PACVOLT**: Gridco's ILPR technology competes directly with traditional step-voltage regulators like Pacific Volt's LVR30/LVR50
- Positions as "next generation" voltage regulation vs. traditional tap-changing regulators
- Targets electric cooperatives, IOUs, and municipal utilities
- Higher cost per unit but claims faster response time and lower maintenance
- Competes with: Pacific Volt, Eaton Cooper Power, Siemens, ABB, Howard Industries (traditional VR manufacturers)

### Relation to Power Utilities & Voltage Regulation
- **CORE BUSINESS** is voltage regulation -- this is Pacific Volt's most direct technology competitor at this conference
- Their power-electronics approach represents a potential paradigm shift from mechanical tap-changing voltage regulation
- Key question for PacVolt: How does Gridco's continuous regulation compare to LVR's stepped regulation in cost, reliability, and field performance?

### Renewable Energy Connections
- Strongly positioned for DER integration -- solar/wind cause rapid voltage fluctuations that benefit from fast power-electronics-based regulation
- Markets heavily to utilities with high solar penetration

### Recent News [VERIFY]

### Online Sentiment [VERIFY]
