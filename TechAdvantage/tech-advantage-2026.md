# TechAdvantage

Greg, Steve S. and I attended the March 2026 TechAdvantage convention in Nashville, TN.  The convention is hosted and organized by the National Rural Electric Cooperative Association (NRECA) for electrical co-ops and related businesses. It's hosted at the Music City Center (just next door to the Country Music Hall of fame) and is one of the nicest convention centers I've been to.

I did end up surveying each of the booths at the expo, looking mainly for people working on 'grid edge' tech, and/or AI related work (mainly to see what people were up to with AI).  I had many good conversations and in most cases gave the economic pitch for the LVRs (8.5 to solve problems, vs. 50k+ infrastructure...) and I couldn't find anyone who disagreed with the economics, and generally people thought this was a useful niche.  However the fact that we didn't find a single other LVR vendor suggests it is perhaps too niche...  Struggled to find anyone to point in a good direction, generally speaking these were mainly salespeople and few engineers.

Back to the furniture discussion, it was on the (completely dead) morning of the last day I decided to make some progress on the firmware code merge. The nice folk at http://dhec.com/ let me use their wonderfully comforable leather lounge chairs to get most of that done.

## Lessons Learned

- Despite not getting the ideal results from the convention, I agree with Steve S.'s point that if you aren't present that people will just assume you don't exist. Companies come and go these days pretty quickly.  
- There were a fair number of changes to the expo floor plan, from the original map on the website, in terms of what companies were present.  I had a list of companies to hit up and at least a third weren't at the booth from the map (and as far as I could tell were just not present).  So there's really a limit on how much research you can usefully do ahead of time.  
- Portability may be a useful sales point.  Do we have any current impediments to portability?  Are there any future improvement that could be made?
- Make sure you have the furniture you need ahead of time, or else get to the rental desk first thing.  We were trying to get a tall chair for Steve and failed as they were out (and it was still fairly early)
